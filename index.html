The Data

The data shown is sourced from the Australian Goverment. It contains details on commercial air travel in 2017 across the nation.
Using this data we can see where people typically fly. Additionally, we can also see how Australia's more rural locations are served.

The data (and therefore the visualisation) does not distinguish directional element in routes, with the data shown representing travel both ways.

Cities are split into three categories. Capital's, Metro, and Regional.
Metro locations are cities with a population larger than 50,000 without being the state capital.

Connections between Locations

Map showing the different routes taken in 2017, by classification.
Hover over a location to view its name and number of connections.

As expected flights are concentrated on the east coast with Perth being a western hub for regional connections.
Interestingly there are no flights from Perth to Alice Springs, or Darwin to Broome. Furthermore the only connection to Uluru is Sydney.

Volume between States

See which interstate trips are the most popular. Volume is measured in people transported.

Given that Sydney and Melbourne are Australia's biggest cities the number of connections between their two states is very high.

It is also of no suprise that the state with the most internal traffic is Queensland, likely due to it containing several Metropolitan locations.

Busiest Airports

The number of flights arriving at and departing from the busiest airports.

It is of no suprise that the airports with the most connections and largest populations have the largest number of flights. Additionally, these airports would also have more international connections.

With 8/10 of the locations on the east coast of the country it is clear where most of Australia's population resides.

Plane size vs Distance

We can investigate how the size of planes used increases with the length of the flight.

It is clear that flights to regional cities are usually short and on small flights. Likely due to limited demand.

However, it can be seen that once a flight of distance 1000km is reached place size typically remains the same except for a few key connections.
(For Reference: Brisbane - Canberra is ~950km)

Flight Capacity Levels

The stacked barcode chart shows the average capacity of each route by their route type.

It can clearly be seen that most flights generally fly with 20% of seats empty. As expected regional flights are often less full.
An outlier can also be identified, the Melbourne to Hamilton Island (Resort Holiday Destination) has an average capacity of 18%.
